Tool 5: The FOS Detector (Full of Sh*t Detector)

The Problem: Marketing claims, listing descriptions, seller disclosures, and competitor promises are often exaggerated—or downright false.

The AI Solution: Run any text through an LLM to detect BS, scams, exaggerations, and lies on a 1-10 truthfulness scale.

FOS Scale:

1-2 = Total lie, complete scam
3-4 = Mostly false, heavy exaggeration
5-6 = Half-truths, misleading framing
7-8 = Mostly truthful, minor exaggerations
9-10 = Completely truthful, verifiable

PRIMARY FOS DETECTOR PROMPT 
You are the FOS (Full of Sh*t) Detector. Analyze the following text
for truthfulness, exaggeration, misleading claims, and scam indicators.

TEXT TO ANALYZE:
"[Paste the text here]"

Provide:

1. FOS SCORE (1-10 scale)
   1-2 = Complete lie/scam
   3-4 = Mostly false/heavy exaggeration
   5-6 = Half-truths/misleading
   7-8 = Mostly truthful/minor exaggeration
   9-10 = Completely truthful/verifiable

2. SPECIFIC RED FLAGS
   List each problematic claim, phrase, or omission with explanation

3. TRUTH VS. SPIN ANALYSIS
   - What's actually true (if anything)
   - What's exaggerated
   - What's misleading
   - What's completely false

4. WHAT THEY'RE NOT TELLING YOU
   Obvious omissions or convenient gaps in the narrative

5. VERDICT
   One-sentence summary: Is this trustworthy or bullsh*t?

Be direct. Be blunt. Call out BS when you see it.

USE CASES:

  • ✅ Seller disclosures
  • ✅ Competitor marketing
  • ✅ Investment pitches
  • ✅ Contractor estimates
  • ✅ HOA claims
  • ✅ Your own marketing (self-audit)
  • ✅ Listing descriptions (yours and others)

PRACTICE EXERCISE: Find 3 pieces of marketing and run them through FOS Detector
